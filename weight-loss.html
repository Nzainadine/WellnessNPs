What it is

A smarter way to lose weight

GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1) is a hormone your body makes that helps regulate appetite, blood sugar and digestion. GLP-1 medications — including semaglutide and tirzepatide — mimic that hormone, helping you feel full sooner and stay full longer, so eating less feels natural rather than forced.

We offer both injectable (a small once-weekly injection) and oral options, and we'll help you choose based on your goals, tolerance and lifestyle. Every plan is prescribed and monitored by a licensed provider and paired with practical nutrition and metabolic support — because medication works best alongside lasting habits.

Questions

GLP-1 FAQ

Oral or injectable — which is better?
Neither is universally "better." Injectables are a once-weekly option many find simple; oral may suit those who prefer no injections. We help you choose based on your goals, tolerance and preferences.
How much weight will I lose?
Results vary by individual, dose and adherence, and we never guarantee a specific number. Your provider sets realistic expectations based on your health profile.
Are these medications FDA-approved?
Some GLP-1 medications are FDA-approved. Compounded versions are not FDA-approved and are prepared and prescribed under the care of a licensed provider. We'll review all options and their differences with you.
Are there side effects?
Like any medication, GLP-1s can cause side effects, most commonly mild digestive symptoms that often ease over time. Your provider reviews these with you and monitors you throughout.
